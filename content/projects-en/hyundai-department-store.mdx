## Approach

### 1) Redesigned Existing Store Analysis Framework into Department Store-Specific Frame

Existing store analysis frameworks assumed single-brand spaces, making direct application difficult for department store environments with tenant stores and multiple entrances.
- Reviewed client questions and on-site structure together to redefine the axes needed for department store analysis.
- Designed analysis structure into 8 categories: `branch revenue`, `vehicle/pedestrian entry`, `promotion/seasonality`, `tenant store purpose visits`, `floor/entrance distribution`, and `comprehensive summary`.

Built an independent analysis system suited to department store environments beyond existing store-centric analysis.

### 2) Converted Executive Questions into Hypothesis-Validation Analysis

What clients wanted were answers to questions like "Did younger visitors increase during Christmas?" and "Did middle-aged proportion grow during holidays?"
- Converted questions into verifiable hypothesis forms by first defining comparable periods and segments.
- Christmas season was compared to same period previous month; holidays separated absolute visitor count from proportion for interpretation; social issues were analyzed alongside weekly sudden-change patterns and entrance-specific entry differences.
- Adjusted interpretation intensity to `rejected`, `partially verified`, and `verified` levels to avoid overstating results.

Created hypothesis-validation analysis documents answering executive questions with data-based evidence.

### 3) Defined Department Store Field Concepts as Custom Events and ESGs

Key operational concepts like vehicle visitors, pedestrian visitors, purpose-visit customers, and zone group movement couldn't be measured with existing events.
- First organized which concepts were actually needed for operational decisions through client discussions, then converted to data definitions.
- Particularly, purpose visits were defined not as customers entering immediately after arrival but as those visiting within 10 minutes of store entry, distinguishing incidental from intentional entry.
- Designed 5 total custom metrics including vehicle/pedestrian separation and zone group visitors.

Department store operational language was converted into a metric system reflectable in actual analysis and dashboards.

### 4) Connected Analysis Results to Executive Reports and Dashboards

No matter how well complex department store data is analyzed, utilization drops if results can't be delivered in formats understandable by both executives and practitioners.
- For executive reporting, structured materials around weekly trends, entrance-specific entry, floor patterns, tenant store purpose visits, and seasonality verification results.
- Simultaneously organized repeatedly-needed metrics into 2 dashboard types, enabling utilization at both department store-wide and tenant store levels.

Analysis results didn't remain confined to report materials but were delivered in forms considering both executive decision-making and practical utilization.
