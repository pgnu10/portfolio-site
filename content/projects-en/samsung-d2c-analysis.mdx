## Enterprise Client Store KPI Design & Validation System

## Project Overview

Samsung D2C stores prioritized brand experience and staff interaction over purchase conversion, unlike general retail — but there were no KPIs or validation criteria to measure this.
Designed 6 D2C-specific KPIs centered on staff-customer interaction, modularized logic to handle requirement changes, and operated a 3-round validation system: consistency check → failure case classification → improvement verification.
Built a behavioral KPI system suited for brand experiential spaces, raised data trust through validation, then connected domestic PoC results to international store expansion.

* Classified and addressed key failure cases: staff contamination, path omission, multi-person mixing
* Authored validation report and delivered to client
* Connected PoC results to international store expansion and other client benchmarks

## Approach

### 1) Designed D2C-Specific KPI System Instead of General Retail KPIs

Samsung D2C stores valued brand experience and staff interaction over purchase conversion, making general store visitor metrics insufficient to explain performance.
- Redefined store value not through simple traffic volume but through `experience/engagement`, `staff response`, and `operational efficiency` perspectives.
- Based on this, designed 6 KPIs including `product experience` and `pickup time`.

Built a KPI system that explains both operations and customer experience for brand experiential spaces that previously lacked measurement standards.

### 2) Separated Logic into Purpose-Based Modules to Handle Frequent Requirement Changes

KPI definitions and detailed logic changed repeatedly during the project; concentrating logic in a single script meant modification scope kept expanding.
- Divided requirements by nature into 4 modules: staff response, product experience, visitors, and exception dwell time handling.
- Designed each module to serve a distinct purpose so that changes to specific KPI definitions wouldn't require modifying the entire pipeline.

Enabled localized modification scope even amid repeated spec changes, making KPI logic maintainable and extensible.

### 3) Built Data Trust Incrementally Through 3-Round Validation Loop

Without a validation system, clients couldn't trust new KPIs, and internally it was difficult to identify which errors recurred.
- Designed validation in 3 stages rather than a single pass.
- Round 1 verified KPI logic and basic consistency; Round 2 categorized failure cases including multi-person mixing, staff contamination, and path omission.
- Round 3 re-verified quality trends through sampling after applying improvement logic.

Creating a validation framework that classifies failure causes and confirms improvement effects enabled raising KPI reliability.

### 4) Connected PoC Results to International Store Expansion

If PoC results remained limited to specific store analysis, the project's value risked being confined to one-time documentation.
- Organized validated KPIs and analysis structures not just as reports but as standards applicable to other stores.

Converted domestic PoC results into reusable analysis assets for international store expansion and other client projects.
