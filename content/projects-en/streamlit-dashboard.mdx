## Approach

### 1) Built Environment for Directly Validating Analysis Ideas Before Product Integration

Even when new analysis perspectives emerged, requesting development directly was costly and documents alone couldn't sufficiently verify actual utility and presentation.
- Chose Streamlit as the experimentation tool, prioritizing validation speed over production polish.
- The key wasn't building many dashboards but rapidly converting analysis ideas into working screens as standards for internal review and discussion.
- Organized frequently recurring question types first, then implemented each as a small prototype with appropriate calculation structures and interactions.

Created a common experimentation foundation for rapidly validating analysis ideas through code and screens rather than verbal explanation.

### 2) Combined External Data for Questions Internal Data Alone Couldn't Answer

When interpreting store visit changes or trade area characteristics, internal visit data alone often couldn't sufficiently explain causes.
- Built a trade area analysis prototype integrating Seoul public data APIs to query living population and trade area data together.
- Configured internal visit patterns and external living area flows to be comparable on a single screen.
- Enabled interpretation of surrounding context beyond just store phenomena.

Rapidly validated the possibility of combining external context for questions internal metrics alone couldn't answer.

### 3) Used Prototypes as Testing Grounds for Follow-Up Operations and Feature Reviews

If prototypes end as one-off demos, they're difficult to retain as organizational assets and disconnected from follow-up projects, their experimental cost exceeds their value.
- In the anomaly detection dashboard, enabled comparison of 9 detectors on the same screen to review which algorithms suit which patterns at the pre-operational stage.
- In the marketing before/after comparison dashboard, provided interpretation criteria like t-test, MDE, and odds ratio to experiment with judgment structures needed for decision-making beyond simple numerical comparison.

Prototypes functioned as validation environments for follow-up system design and operational judgment, beyond one-off experiments.
